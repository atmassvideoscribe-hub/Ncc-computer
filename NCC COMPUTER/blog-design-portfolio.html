Design Portfolio Checklist

Your portfolio is your most powerful tool as a designer. Here's everything you need to create a portfolio that stands out and lands you opportunities.

Essential Elements

✓ Strong Introduction

Start with a clear, concise bio that explains who you are, what you do, and what makes you unique. Include a professional photo and your design philosophy in 2-3 sentences.

✓ 5-8 Quality Projects

Quality over quantity! Showcase your best work only. Each project should demonstrate different skills and problem-solving approaches. Include both personal and client work if possible.

✓ Case Studies

For each project, explain:
• The problem you were solving
• Your design process and thinking
• Tools and techniques used
• The final outcome and impact
• Mockups and prototypes

✓ Visual Consistency

Use a consistent layout, typography, and color scheme throughout your portfolio. Your portfolio itself is a design project—make it beautiful and easy to navigate.

Technical Requirements

  • Mobile-responsive design
  • Fast loading times (optimize images!)
  • Easy navigation with clear sections
  • Contact information easily accessible
  • PDF download option for offline viewing
  • Links to your social media and Behance/Dribbble

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Showing too many mediocre projects
  • ❌ Using generic stock photos without context
  • ❌ No explanation of your design process
  • ❌ Outdated or broken links
  • ❌ Cluttered, hard-to-navigate layout
  • ❌ Missing contact information

Platform Recommendations

Build Your Own: WordPress, Webflow, or custom HTML/CSS
Portfolio Platforms: Behance, Dribbble, Adobe Portfolio
Hosting: Netlify or GitHub Pages (free options)
